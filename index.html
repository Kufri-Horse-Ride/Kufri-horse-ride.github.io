The Journey: What to Expect

The ride begins at the Kufri parking area or the main market and takes you up to Mahasu Peak, the highest point in Kufri (2,720 meters).

  • The Trail: The path is approximately 2 to 3 kilometers long. While it can be hiked, the terrain is often muddy and slippery (especially in winter and monsoon), making the horse ride a practical necessity rather than just a fun activity.
  • Duration: It typically takes 30 to 40 minutes to reach the top.
  • The Views: As your horse climbs the winding path, you will see the dense reserve forest on one side and the sprawling valley on the other. On clear days, the peak offers spectacular views of the Badrinath and Kedarnath ranges.

Watch Out for the "Points" Scam

When booking your ride, handlers may offer you packages covering "5 Points" or "8 Points" (like Apple Garden, Shooting Point, Valley View, etc.).

  • The Reality: Most of these "points" are simply stops along the same single path to the peak.
  • Our Advice: Do not pay extra for "more points." Simply ask for a standard Kufri horse ride to the top of Mahasu Peak and back. You will naturally see all these views on the way up.

Essential Safety & Travel Tips

To ensure your adventure is safe and enjoyable, keep these expert tips in mind:

  • Footwear Matters: The trail is full of mule dung and slush. If you don't have waterproof trekking shoes, rent gumboots from the local shops before the ride.
  • Back Pain Alert: The ride can be jerky. If you have severe back issues or are pregnant, it is safer to skip the ride and enjoy the views from the base.
  • Check the Weather: Rides may be suspended during heavy snowstorms or torrential rain for safety.
  • Hold Tight: Always hold the saddle handle firmly, especially during the steep ascent and descent.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Kufri horse ride safe for children?
Yes, thousands of children enjoy the ride every year. The ponies are well-trained and accustomed to the path. For very small children, a handler will walk alongside the horse to ensure safety.
Can we walk to Mahasu Peak instead of taking a horse?
Technically, yes, you can hike. However, the path is shared with hundreds of horses, making it extremely muddy and slushy. Walking can be difficult and messy, which is why most tourists prefer the horse ride.
What is the best time to visit for the ride?
The ride is open year-round! Visit in Winter (Dec-Feb) for a magical snowy experience, or Summer (April-June) for pleasant weather and clear views of the Himalayas.
